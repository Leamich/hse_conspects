$$
\begin{array}{l}
\int a \, dx =ax+c\\
\int x^{p} \, dx = \frac{x^{p+1}}{p+1}+c,\ p\ne -1\\
\int  \frac{dx}{x} = \ln|x|+c\\
\int a^{x} \, dx =\frac{a^{x}}{\ln a}+c,\ a>0,\ a\ne 1\\
\int \sin x \, dx =-\cos x+c\\
\int  \cos x \, dx =\sin x+c\\
\int \frac{dx}{x^{2}+1}=\arctg x+c\\
\int  \, \frac{dx}{\sqrt{1-x^{2}}}=\arcsin x+c
\end{array} 
$$
